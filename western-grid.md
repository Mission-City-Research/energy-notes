Made up of the following states: Arizona, California, Colorado, Idaho, Montana, Nevada, New Mexico, Oregon,  Utah, Washington and Wyoming

The #Western Grid has dramatically changed its  Fuel mix for electricity generation over the last decade. Coal has decreased substantially  and has been  replaced by solar and wind.

## Key Observations

- Total Western coal capacity declines from **~31.8 GW (2015)** to **~20.9 GW (2025)**  
  → a reduction of roughly **34%** over the decade.
- Major step-downs occur around **2019–2021**, driven by large unit retirements.
- **Oregon and Idaho reach zero coal capacity** by the early 2020s.
- **Wyoming remains the largest coal state**, but still experiences material declines post-2022.
- Capacity changes align with known retirements, such as:
  - Navajo Generating Station (AZ)
  - San Juan Generating Station (NM)
  - Boardman (OR)
  - Centralia (WA)
